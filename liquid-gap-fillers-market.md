<p><p>The liquid gap fillers market is primarily divided into two main types: silicone and non-silicone types. Silicone gap fillers are known for their excellent thermal stability, flexibility, and resistance to moisture, making them suitable for high-performance applications such as electronics and automotive. Non-silicone gap fillers, often based on materials like polyurethane or epoxy, provide cost-effective solutions with varying mechanical properties, suitable for general-purpose applications. The choice between these two types depends on specific application requirements, such as thermal conductivity and chemical resistance.</p></p>

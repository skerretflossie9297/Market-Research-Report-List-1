<p><p>The Fiber Reinforced Plastic (FRP) tanks market is primarily segmented into vertical and horizontal types. Vertical FRP tanks are cylindrical structures designed to hold liquids and are typically used in applications with limited ground space. They facilitate gravity drainage and offer ease of access for maintenance. Horizontal FRP tanks, on the other hand, are wider and lower to the ground, allowing for easier loading and unloading of contents. Both types leverage the benefits of corrosion resistance and lightweight characteristics of FRP materials.</p></p>

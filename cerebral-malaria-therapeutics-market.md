<p><p>The cerebral malaria therapeutics market primarily comprises two main types: quinine and artemisinin derivatives. Quinine, an alkaloid derived from cinchona bark, has been used for centuries to treat malaria but may have side effects and resistance issues. Artemisinin derivatives, derived from the sweet wormwood plant, are more effective and have a quicker action against the malaria parasite. This market plays a crucial role in addressing severe malaria cases and adapting to emerging resistance trends within the parasite populations.</p></p>

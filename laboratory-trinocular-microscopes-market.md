<p><p>Laboratory trinocular microscopes are categorized mainly into inverted and upright types. Inverted microscopes are designed for examining samples in liquid, making them ideal for cell cultures and biological studies, as the objective lenses are positioned above the stage. Conversely, upright microscopes are versatile for traditional sample viewing, supporting various applications in materials science and biology, where samples are usually placed on the stage. Both types offer trinocular capabilities for improved imaging and documentation.</p></p>

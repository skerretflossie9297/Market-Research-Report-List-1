<p><ul><li>Electric Socket</li><li>Ultrasonic</li><li>Hot Plate</li><li>Spin</li><li>Hot Gas</li><li>Extrusion</li><li>Injection</li><li>High Frequency</li><li>Laser</li><li>Others</li></ul></p>
<p><p>The plastic welding equipment market encompasses various technologies, each suited for specific applications. Electric socket welding involves connecting pipes using heated sockets. Ultrasonic welding utilizes high-frequency vibrations to join materials. Hot plate welding employs heated plates for fusion. Spin welding involves rotational motion for strong bonds. Hot gas welding uses a heated gas stream to melt surfaces. Extrusion welding fuses thermoplastics through melted material, while injection welding uses force to mold plastics. High-frequency and laser welding utilize electromagnetic fields and focused light, respectively, for precise joins. Other methods include a range of specialized techniques catering to diverse industrial needs.</p></p>

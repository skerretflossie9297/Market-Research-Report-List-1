<p><p>The ultrafilter membranes market is primarily divided into two types: inorganic and organic membranes. Inorganic membranes are typically made from ceramic or metal materials, offering high thermal stability, chemical resistance, and durability, making them suitable for demanding applications in industries like petrochemicals and wastewater treatment. In contrast, organic membranes are derived from polymeric materials, providing flexibility and ease of production. They are widely used in food processing and pharmaceuticals due to their efficient separation capabilities and cost-effectiveness.</p></p>

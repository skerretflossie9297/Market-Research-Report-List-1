<p><ul><li>Hydrogenated Vegetable Oil (HVO)</li><li>Fischer-Tropsch (FT)</li></ul></p>
<p><p>The aviation biofuels market includes two main types: Hydrogenated Vegetable Oil (HVO) and Fischer-Tropsch (FT) fuels. HVO is produced by hydrogenating vegetable oils or animal fats, resulting in a drop-in fuel compatible with existing jet engines. Fischer-Tropsch fuels are synthesized from biomass or natural gas through gasification, then converted into liquid hydrocarbons. Both types aim to reduce carbon emissions while offering renewable alternatives to conventional fossil fuels, thus supporting the aviation industry's sustainability goals.</p></p>

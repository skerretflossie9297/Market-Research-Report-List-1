<p><p>The measles vaccine market primarily comprises two types: inactivated vaccines and attenuated vaccines. Inactivated vaccines contain killed virus particles, prompting an immune response without causing disease, but may require boosters for long-term immunity. Attenuated vaccines, on the other hand, use a weakened form of the virus, providing a strong and lasting immune response, often in a single dose. The demand for these vaccines is influenced by factors like public health initiatives, outbreak responses, and vaccination programs worldwide.</p></p>

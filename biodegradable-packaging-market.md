<p><ul><li>Natural</li><li>Synthetic</li></ul></p>
<p><p>The biodegradable packaging market is primarily divided into two types: natural and synthetic. Natural biodegradable packaging is derived from organic materials, such as plant-based polymers, which decompose easily in the environment. This type includes materials like starch, cellulose, and polylactic acid (PLA). On the other hand, synthetic biodegradable packaging is made from petrochemical sources but designed to break down more rapidly than conventional plastics. These materials often incorporate additives that enhance biodegradability, striking a balance between durability and environmental responsibility.</p></p>

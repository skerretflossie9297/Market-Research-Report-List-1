<p><p>The viral vectors market encompasses several types, each with unique applications in gene therapy and biomedical research. Retroviral vectors integrate into host DNA, offering stable gene expression, while lentiviral vectors can transduce non-dividing cells. Adenoviral vectors provide high transduction efficiency but transient expression. Herpes simplex virus vectors are suited for large transgenes and neuronal targeting. Chimeric viral vectors combine features of different viruses for tailored delivery. Other vectors include adeno-associated, vesicular stomatitis, and baculovirus vectors, each serving specific therapeutic needs.</p></p>

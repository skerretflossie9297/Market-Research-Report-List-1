<p><p>The nucleic vaccine market is divided into two main types: preventive and therapeutic nucleic vaccines. Preventive nucleic vaccines are designed to elicit an immune response to prevent infectious diseases, such as COVID-19 or influenza. In contrast, therapeutic nucleic vaccines aim to treat existing conditions, including cancer, by stimulating the immune system to target and eliminate disease cells. Both types leverage genetic material, like DNA or RNA, to instruct cells in the body to produce antigens that trigger immune responses.</p></p>

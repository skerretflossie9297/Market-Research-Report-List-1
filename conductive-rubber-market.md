<p><strong>The Conductive Rubber Market Analysis by types is segmented into:</strong></p>
<p><ul><li>Type I</li><li>Type II</li></ul></p>
<p><p>Conductive rubber is categorized into two main types based on its composition and application: Type I and Type II. Type I conductive rubber typically consists of a blend of rubber and conductive materials, designed for applications requiring efficient electrostatic discharge (ESD) protection and grounding. In contrast, Type II combines insulating rubber with conductive ingredients, offering moderate conductivity while maintaining structural integrity. Both types serve various industries, including electronics and automotive, where electromagnetic interference (EMI) shielding and static control are essential.</p></p>

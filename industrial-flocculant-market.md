<p><p>The industrial flocculant market is categorized primarily into organic and inorganic coagulants. Organic coagulants are derived from natural sources, such as vegetative materials or synthetic polymers, and are known for their effectiveness in facilitating sedimentation and reducing sludge volume in wastewater treatment. Inorganic coagulants, on the other hand, typically include metal salts like aluminum and ferric compounds, which are efficient in neutralizing charges in particulates and promoting faster floc formation, often used in various industrial processes to enhance water clarity and quality.</p></p>
